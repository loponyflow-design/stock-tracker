# Amazon.com, Inc. 10-K FY2025 (excerpt — investment summary)

## ธุรกิจหลัก
Amazon operates through three segments: North America (online & physical retail), International (overseas e-commerce), and AWS (cloud computing). The company is customer-centric, selling hundreds of millions of products directly and via third-party sellers, offering advertising services, providing cloud infrastructure to enterprises, and manufacturing electronic devices. AWS is the high-margin profit engine while retail segments compete on selection, price, and convenience.

## Product lines
- Online retail (product and digital content)
- Physical stores (Amazon Fresh, Whole Foods, bookstores)
- Third-party seller services (commissions, logistics)
- Advertising services (sponsored ads, display, video)
- Subscription services (Amazon Prime, digital media)
- AWS compute, storage, database, analytics, AI/ML services
- Electronic devices (Kindle, Fire, Echo, Ring, eero, Alexa)
- Healthcare services

## Distribution
North America and International segments operate fulfillment networks across geographies plus co-sourced/outsourced arrangements, physical stores, and digital delivery. AWS serves developers and enterprises globally via direct sales channels.

## การแข่งขัน
Amazon faces intense competition across multiple industries: e-commerce (Walmart, Alibaba, Target), cloud (Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle), advertising (Google, Meta), and electronics (Samsung, Apple). Competitive factors: selection, price, speed/reliability of service, and technology. Some competitors have greater resources, brand recognition, or regional advantages; however, Amazon's scale, fulfillment infrastructure, and AWS dominance provide structural moats.

## Supply chain — ความเสี่ยง
- Dependence on inventory management and fulfillment execution; operational disruptions could impact sales
- China-based suppliers (electronics, components) and Chinese third-party sellers expose Amazon to tariffs, trade restrictions, and geopolitical risks
- International operations subject to regulatory changes, labor unions, currency fluctuations, and different consumer behaviors
- Rapid technology investment (especially AI infrastructure) increases capex requirements and depreciation expense
- Labor market tightness in tech (software engineers, cloud specialists) and fulfillment (warehouse workers)

## Seasonality
Q4 (October–December) is peak season historically due to holiday shopping. Amazon adjusts inventory, staffing, and capex planning accordingly. Q1–Q3 are relatively lower-volume periods.

## Human capital
As of December 31, 2025: approximately **1,576,000 full-time and part-time employees**. Competition for qualified technical personnel (software engineers, AI/ML specialists) remains intense. Amazon invests in employee development (Career Choice program with 300k+ participants), competitive compensation, benefits, and safety improvements.

## IP
Amazon relies on trademarks (Amazon, Alexa, Prime), copyrights (video content, software), patents on technology and logistics, domain names, and trade secrets. Filed numerous U.S. and international patent applications covering proprietary technology.

## FY2025 Financial Summary

### Revenue drivers
- **North America (+10.0%):** Increased unit sales, third-party seller services, advertising (Prime Video ads expansion), and subscription growth offset by fulfillment/shipping cost pressures
- **International (+13.3%):** Strong growth in UK, Germany, Japan from increased e-commerce adoption; positive forex impact ($4.9B) partially offset by geopolitical headwinds in some markets
- **AWS (+19.8%):** Increased customer usage and AI adoption drove growth; long-term contract pricing changes slightly offset gains; continued investment in technology infrastructure

## Key Risk Factors

### Macro & geopolitical
- **Economic sensitivity:** E-commerce retail segments depend on consumer spending; recession or inflation could reduce unit volume and/or force pricing pressure
- **Trade & tariffs:** Tariffs on imports (especially from China for electronics and goods) could raise COGS or necessitate price increases
- **Currency fluctuations:** International revenue subject to forex volatility (FX benefited International operating income by $903M in FY2025, but negatively impacted North America by $204M and AWS by $341M)
- **China & India regulatory:** PRC and India restrict foreign ownership in retail and cloud services; regulatory changes could force restructuring or license revocation
- **Geopolitical events:** War, terrorism, or trade wars could disrupt supply chains or customer demand

### Regulatory & legal
- **Antitrust & competition:** Governments worldwide are scrutinizing Amazon's market position (e-commerce, AWS, advertising); regulations could limit data use, bundling practices, or pricing control
- **Data privacy & cybersecurity:** GDPR, CCPA, and emerging privacy laws increase compliance costs and liability risks; data breaches could harm reputation and trigger penalties
- **Labor & unions:** Growing union organizing efforts in warehouses could increase wage pressures and reduce operational flexibility; labor disputes could disrupt fulfillment
- **Content & IP liability:** Amazon faces claims from publishers, authors, and product sellers regarding IP infringement, counterfeits, and product liability
- **Tax regulations:** Complex global tax rules and ongoing tax disputes (e.g., Italy settlements of $1.1B in FY2025) create uncertainty and potential back-tax exposure

### Business risks
- **Competition & market share:** Intense competition from well-capitalized competitors (Walmart, Microsoft, Google, Chinese e-commerce platforms) could erode margins or market share
- **Technology obsolescence & AI:** Rapid AI/ML adoption requires sustained heavy capex investment in data centers and talent; failure to compete in AI could weaken cloud services
- **Supply chain disruption:** Dependence on third-party logistics, fulfillment networks, and supplier relationships; disruptions (shipping delays, component shortages) could impact sales and costs
- **Customer concentration:** Third-party sellers and AWS customers are significant revenue sources; loss of major customers or merchants could impact growth
- **Retail profitability:** North America and International retail segments operate on thin margins; inability to scale profitably or maintain customer loyalty could materially reduce earnings
- **AWS competition:** Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud aggressively compete on pricing and features; AWS market share loss could reduce high-margin revenue
- **Reputational risks:** Environmental sustainability commitments, AI ethics, labor practices, and content moderation challenges could harm brand and attract regulatory scrutiny

## Investment Takeaway

**Bullish signals:**
1. AWS continues double-digit growth (19.8%) with improving operating leverage; AI demand driving infrastructure investment
2. Advertising business accelerating (+22.1%), capturing higher-margin revenue from seller/vendor base
3. North America retail achieving consistent 10% growth with margin expansion
4. Strong FCF generation ($38.2B) despite heavy capex, enabling reinvestment and potential future returns
5. Consolidated operating margin expanding (11.2% vs. 10.7%) even as company scales

**Bearish/watch factors:**
1. **Capex intensity:** $131.8B capex (18.4% of revenue) is unprecedented; AWS returns on this investment may take time to materialize
2. **Retail margin pressure:** North America and International retail still operate at thin margins; dependent on volume growth and automation
3. **Legal/tax headwinds:** $5B+ in FY2025 for settlements and disputes; elevated non-operating items
4. **Competition:** AWS faces aggressive pricing/feature competition; retail faces Amazon's own seller competition and omnichannel rivals
5. **China exposure:** Significant reliance on China-based suppliers and sellers; tariff/geopolitical risk remains
6. **Valuation & execution:** Heavy AI infrastructure bet requires execution; near-term ROI uncertain

**For long-term investors:** Amazon offers a portfolio of high-growth (AWS, advertising) and mature (retail) businesses, strong cash generation, and significant competitive moats. Concerns center on capex efficiency, macro sensitivity of retail, and regulatory scrutiny.
