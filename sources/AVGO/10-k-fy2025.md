# Broadcom 10-K FY2025 (excerpt — investment summary)

## ธุรกิจหลัก
Broadcom designs, develops, and supplies semiconductor and infrastructure software solutions. The company operates two segments: Semiconductor Solutions (AI accelerators, networking, wireless connectivity, storage, broadband) and Infrastructure Software (private cloud, mainframe, cybersecurity, enterprise software, SAN management). Products are sold to data center operators, cloud providers, networking equipment makers, smartphone/device manufacturers, and enterprise IT customers worldwide.

## Product lines
**Semiconductor Solutions:**
- Custom AI accelerators (ASICs) and AI networking products (strong growth driver)
- Ethernet NIC controllers for data center and machine learning
- Fiber optic components for networking and storage
- RF front-end modules and Wi-Fi/Bluetooth combo chips for wireless devices
- Broadband and industrial connectivity solutions

**Infrastructure Software:**
- VMware Cloud Foundation (private cloud, VCF) — primary growth driver
- VMware vSphere Foundation and vSAN (hypervisor and storage software)
- Mainframe software (AIOps, DevOps, cybersecurity)
- Enterprise cybersecurity and compliance solutions
- Telco Cloud Platform for 5G operators
- VMware Private AI for generative AI with data compliance

## Segments
**Semiconductor Solutions:** $36,858M (FY2025, +22% YoY) — 58% of revenue
**Infrastructure Software:** $27,029M (FY2025, +26% YoY) — 42% of revenue

## Distribution
Direct sales to major enterprise customers and cloud operators; distribution through broad global network of component distributors serving OEMs (especially in Asia for manufacturing). Direct relationships with top cloud hyperscalers and telecom operators.

## การแข่งขัน
Market position: Leading provider of custom AI accelerators and networking silicon for data center. Strong market share in Ethernet networking, Wi-Fi/Bluetooth. Faces competition from AMD, Intel, NVIDIA (GPU), Qualcomm, Broadcom internal erosion from tablet/device decline. Infrastructure software highly competitive vs. AWS, Microsoft, Nutanix. Differentiation through high-performance AI/networking silicon and VMware's large enterprise base.

## Supply chain — ความเสี่ยง
- Dependent on contract manufacturers (TSMC, Samsung, others) for fabrication
- Supply chain realignment in progress to shift production and IP to US (supply chain resilience)
- Concentrated customer base risk (loss of major customer could adversely impact revenue)
- Geopolitical risk: significant revenue exposure to China (directly and through customers); tariffs, export controls, CFIUS scrutiny
- Cyclical semiconductor industry exposed to data center capex volatility
- Component shortages and cost inflation risk

## Seasonality
Not explicitly stated in document; however, data center capex and cloud infrastructure typically show seasonal patterns (Q4 strength).

## Human capital
**Employees:** 33,000 worldwide as of Nov 2, 2025
- 57% in research and development roles
- Geographic distribution: 49% North America, 36% Asia, 15% EMEA
- Voluntary attrition rate: 4.1% (below technology industry benchmark)
- Benefits: 401(k) with dollar-for-dollar match up to 6%; defined benefit and contribution retirement plans internationally

## IP
- ~19,000 U.S. and other patents; 2,170 pending applications
- Patent expiration dates: 2025–2044; no material patents expiring soon
- Not dependent on any single patent or group of related patents
- Focus on patenting inventions likely to be incorporated into products

## FY2025 Financial Summary

### Revenue drivers
- **Semiconductor Solutions +22% YoY ($36.9B):** Strong demand for custom AI accelerators and AI networking products (primary driver)
- **Infrastructure Software +26% YoY ($27.0B):** Strong demand for VMware Cloud Foundation (VCF); shift to subscription licensing model; lower integration costs post-VMware acquisition
- **Total revenue +24% YoY ($63.9B):** 
  - Products: $44.8B (+28% YoY)
  - Subscriptions/services: $19.0B (+14% YoY)

### Gross margin
- FY2025: 68% (up from 63% FY2024) — driven by higher software revenue mix and strong AI-related semiconductor demand
- FY2024: 63%
- FY2023: 69%
- Drivers: Higher software revenue (higher margin), integration cost savings from VMware, pricing power on AI semiconductor products

### Operating expenses
**R&D:** $10,977M (17% of revenue) — up $1,667M (+18%) due to higher stock-based compensation and AI development
**SG&A:** $4,211M (7% of revenue) — down $748M (-15%) due to lower compensation (headcount reduction), lower VMware integration costs
**Amortization of acquisition intangibles:** $2,031M — down $1,213M due to full amortization of customer-related intangibles from prior software acquisitions
**Restructuring charges:** $591M — down $942M (-61%) as VMware integration nears completion

### Liquidity & capital return
**Cash:** $16,178M (up $6,830M from prior year) — strong operating cash flow conversion
**Debt:** $65,136M total ($3,152M short-term, $61,984M long-term) — net debt position of $48,958M
**Borrowings FY2025:** $15,666M in new long-term debt; $18,478M debt repayment
**Dividends:** $11,142M paid (FY2025), $9,814M (FY2024) — consistent capital returns
**Share repurchases:** $2,450M (formal program) + $3,860M (tax withholdings) = $6,310M total (down from $12,392M FY2024)

### Tax
**FY2025:** Benefit of $397M (-1.7% rate) — unusual benefit from uncertain tax benefit recognition (statute of limitation expirations, audit settlements) and excess tax benefits from stock awards, offset by valuation allowance on Corporate Alternative Minimum Tax credits
**FY2024:** Provision of $3,748M (37.8% rate) — elevated due to non-recurring intra-group IP transfer to US and shift in jurisdictional mix of income
**FY2023:** Provision of $1,015M (6.7% rate)

## Key Risk Factors

### Macro & geopolitical
- Highly cyclical semiconductor industry undergoing profound change due to AI; data center capex volatility
- China revenue concentration (direct and indirect through customers); exposure to China import/export restrictions and tariffs
- Global political and economic uncertainty; international operations complexity
- CFIUS and export control scrutiny on AI semiconductor sales and foreign participation

### Regulatory & legal
- Antitrust and monopoly risks from market concentration
- IP litigation and patent disputes
- Cybersecurity vulnerability liability (software portfolio security risks)
- Data privacy and compliance obligations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

### Business risks
- **Customer concentration:** Significant revenue from limited number of major customers; loss of major customer could materially impact revenue
- **Supply chain:** Dependent on limited contract manufacturers (TSMC, Samsung); supply chain realignment in progress adds integration risk
- **Competition:** Intense competition from AMD, Intel, NVIDIA, Qualcomm; subscription model transition challenges
- **Integration:** VMware integration ongoing; realization of expected financial benefits uncertain
- **Technology obsolescence:** Rapid technology change; R&D investment effectiveness uncertain
- **Talent retention:** Highly competitive labor market; engineering talent critical to success
