# Neurocrine Biosciences 10-K FY2025 (excerpt — investment summary)

## ธุรกิจหลัก

Neurocrine Biosciences is a neuroscience-focused biopharmaceutical company dedicated to discovering, developing, and commercializing life-changing treatments for patients with under-addressed neurological, psychiatric, endocrine, and immunological disorders. The company generates revenue primarily from FDA-approved medications and is expanding its pipeline across multiple therapeutic areas. Core product portfolio includes INGREZZA (valbenazine) for tardive dyskinesia and Huntington's chorea, and CRENESSITY (crinecerfont) for congenital adrenal hyperplasia.

## Product lines

- **INGREZZA (valbenazine)** — FDA-approved for tardive dyskinesia (TD), chorea associated with Huntington's disease; marketed as DYSVAL in Japan and REMLEAS in select Asian markets (Tanabe Pharma handles ex-US rights in Asia)
- **CRENESSITY (crinecerfont)** — First-in-class FDA-approved CRF-1 receptor antagonist for classic congenital adrenal hyperplasia (CAH); approved December 2024
- **Elagolix (co-marketed with AbbVie)** — Approved for endometriosis and uterine fibroids; AbbVie retains global commercialization rights
- **Pipeline candidates** — Phase 3: osavampator (AMPA receptor potentiator for major depressive disorder), direclidine (M4 receptor agonist for schizophrenia); Phase 2 and Phase 1 programs across psychiatry, neurology, endocrinology, and immunology

## Segments

Single global business segment — operates as unified pharmaceutical company with revenue primarily from U.S. market. Domestic operations generate significant income; foreign operations currently show losses. Geographic revenue split: Predominantly U.S. focused with limited international presence via licensing partnerships.

## Distribution

- **INGREZZA:** Exclusive U.S. distribution through limited specialty pharmacy network, select specialty pharmacy providers, wholesale distributors, and specialty distributors. Specialty sales force of ~600 professionals focused on neurology, psychiatry, long-term care, and rare diseases.
- **CRENESSITY:** U.S. distribution through single specialty pharmacy provider reflecting rare disease focus and high-touch model.
- **Third-party logistics** — Uses specialized logistics providers for packaging, warehousing, and shipment to ensure broad patient access.

## การแข่งขัน

- **INGREZZA** competes with AUSTEDO (deutetrabenazine, Teva), XENAZINE (tetrabenazine), generic equivalents, antipsychotics (clozapine), anticholinergics, benzodiazepines, and botulinum toxin for TD treatment. Strong market position as first-to-market for TD; patent protection extends through 2027–2040 with patent term extension on key compound (expires 2031).
- **CRENESSITY** competes with high-dose corticosteroid monotherapy (current standard of care); 20+ steroid manufacturers and several CAH development programs in pipeline. Orphan drug exclusivity provides 7-year protection.
- Competition from academic institutions, biotech, and pharma firms across pipeline candidates in psychiatry, neurology, endocrinology.

## Supply chain — ความเสี่ยง

- Fully outsourced manufacturing — no internal manufacturing; relies on third-party contract manufacturers for INGREZZA, CRENESSITY, and candidates. Long-term commercial supply agreements with multiple manufacturers mitigate single-source risk.
- API and raw materials sourced from various third-party suppliers. Risk of manufacturing delays, supply chain disruptions, quality control issues, or supplier failure could impact product availability and revenue.
- Dependent on third-party logistics for warehousing and shipment; FDA/cGMP compliance oversight required for all manufacturers.

## Seasonality

Not explicitly disclosed in 10-K. No apparent quarter-to-quarter pattern called out; revenue likely driven by patient adoption and prescription patterns rather than seasonal demand for these specialty pharmaceuticals.

## Human capital

Sales force: ~600 professionals across U.S. (announced expansion of INGREZZA and CRENESSITY sales teams in October 2025, expected completion by Q1 2026). Total employee count not explicitly disclosed in 10-K. Company emphasizes challenges in recruiting and retaining qualified scientists, clinical, sales, marketing, and administrative personnel in competitive pharma market.

## IP

- **INGREZZA:** 22 issued FDA Orange Book-listed U.S. patents expiring 2027–2040; one patent (8,039,627) received 552-day patent term extension, now expires 2031. Settlement agreements with ANDA filers allow generic entry on March 1, 2038 or earlier under certain circumstances.
- **CRENESSITY:** 4 issued FDA Orange Book-listed U.S. patents expiring 2035–2041; pending applications could extend exclusivity to 2046. Seven years FDA orphan drug exclusivity.
- Additional patent portfolio covering pipeline candidates, formulations, methods of administration, delivery technologies, and manufacturing processes.

## Key Risk Factors

### Macro & geopolitical
- Biopharmaceutical industry subject to macroeconomic and healthcare spending cycles; regulatory policy changes (FDA actions, Medicare drug negotiation program) could affect pricing and reimbursement.
- INGREZZA qualifies for small biotech exception from Medicare negotiation program (exemption through 2027; negotiation potential for 2029 onwards).
- Reimbursement and coverage uncertainty; no guarantee payors will maintain or expand coverage and reimbursement levels for INGREZZA, CRENESSITY, or pipeline candidates.

### Regulatory & legal
- FDA approval required for all new indications and formulations; post-approval requirements include Phase 4 trials, surveillance, labeling restrictions, potential withdrawal.
- Product liability, patent litigation, IP disputes (e.g., generic ANDA settlements, trade secret misappropriation claims).
- Outsourced manufacturing dependent on third-party cGMP compliance; FDA inspections and quality issues could disrupt supply.
- INGREZZA patent settlements allow generic competition beginning March 1, 2038 (or earlier under specified conditions).

### Business risks
- INGREZZA and CRENESSITY commercial success dependent on physician and patient acceptance, sales force effectiveness, reimbursement, and continued clinical validation.
- Pipeline execution risk — Phase 3 programs (osavampator in MDD, direclidine in schizophrenia) critical for long-term growth; late-stage failures would damage momentum.
- Competitive threats from AUSTEDO (deutetrabenazine for TD), steroid-based CAH therapies, and numerous pipeline programs in psychiatry/neurology.
- Key personnel retention and recruitment in competitive biotech labor market.
- Third-party manufacturer dependence; single-source or supply chain risks could impact INGREZZA/CRENESSITY availability and revenue.
- Cybersecurity and remote work risks, especially for R&D and clinical data.

## Strengths (Investment perspective)

1. **Dominant market position:** INGREZZA is market leader in TD treatment with strong patent protection (expires 2027–2040); 8.6% YoY revenue growth showing sustained demand and pricing power.
2. **Recent successful product launch:** CRENESSITY (approved December 2024) generated $301.2M in first-year revenue, demonstrating commercial execution and market potential for rare disease therapies.
3. **Strong cash generation:** Operating CF of $782.7M and FCF of $748.7M (26.2% of revenue) provide capital for R&D, buybacks, and pipeline expansion without leverage.
4. **Robust pipeline:** Late-stage psychiatry candidates (osavampator for MDD, direclidine for schizophrenia) offer near-term catalysts; Phase 3 data expected 2027. Expansion into endocrinology (obesity via CRF-2) and biologics broadens TAM.
5. **High gross margins:** 98.2% gross margin reflects specialty pharma economics and pricing power.

---

## Risks/Concerns (Investment perspective)

1. **INGREZZA patent cliff:** Generic competition begins March 1, 2038 (or earlier); 13-year runway is long but risk looms. Company must establish new revenue drivers (new indications, CRENESSITY, pipeline).
2. **CRENESSITY unproven:** New product (launch Dec 2024); market adoption, competitive steroid therapies, and pricing pressure uncertain. CAH is rare disease with limited patient pool (~20k U.S. patients).
3. **Pipeline execution risk:** Phase 3 programs (osavampator, direclidine) are critical; CNS failures are common. Late-stage negative data could reset valuation.
4. **Rising R&D spending:** R&D expenses increased 39% YoY to $1,015.7M; if pipeline slows, profitability could compress.
5. **Competition:** AUSTEDO (Teva) and off-label therapies limit INGREZZA pricing; CA steroid manufacturers compete with CRENESSITY.
6. **Medicare negotiation risk:** INGREZZA has small biotech exemption through 2027; if selected for negotiation post-2028, pricing pressure could occur.

6. What is the total addressable market (TAM) for CRF-2 agonist (obesity) opportunity (NBIP-2118)? Competitive landscape vs. GLP-1 agents?
7. How is the company managing manufacturing risk for CRENESSITY and INGREZZA given full outsourcing? Any single-source dependencies?
8. What is the patient acquisition cost and lifetime value for INGREZZA and CRENESSITY? Payback period for commercial expansion announced in October 2025?
