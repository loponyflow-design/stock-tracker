# Nordson Corporation 10-K FY2025 (excerpt — investment summary)

## ธุรกิจหลัก
Nordson is a precision technology company that engineers, manufactures and markets differentiated products and systems used for precision dispensing, applying and controlling of adhesives, coatings, polymers, sealants, biomaterials, and other fluids; test and inspect for quality; and treat and cure surfaces. Serves diverse end markets including medical, electronics, packaging, automotive, consumer non-durable, industrial, and precision agriculture. Approximately 67% of revenue generated outside the United States, with global direct sales and service network in 35+ countries.

## Product lines
- **Industrial Precision Solutions (IPS):** Industrial coatings, nonwovens, packaging, polymer processing, precision agriculture, product assembly
- **Medical and Fluid Solutions (MFS):** Engineered fluid solutions, medical interventional solutions (catheters, balloons, stents), medical fluid components, surgical solutions
- **Advanced Technology Solutions (ATS):** Electronics dispense systems (semiconductors, PCBs, electronics), test & inspection (optical, X-ray, acoustic microscopy), measurement and control technologies

## Segments
**Revenue by Segment (FY2025):**
- Industrial Precision Solutions (IPS): $1,331.8M (47.7%)
- Medical and Fluid Solutions (MFS): $835.4M (29.9%)
- Advanced Technology Solutions (ATS): $624.5M (22.4%)
- Total: $2,791.7M

**Geographic (FY2025):**
- Americas: $1,205.8M (43.2%) — mostly US
- Europe: $722.2M (25.9%)
- Asia Pacific: $863.6M (30.9%)
- International: 66.9% of total sales

## Distribution
Direct global sales force and qualified distributors in 35+ countries. No single customer accounted for 10%+ of sales in 2025. Majority revenue from point-in-time recognition at shipment; <10% from over-time performance obligations.

## การแข่งขัน
Operates in competitive global market competing with large, well-established manufacturers. Competitive advantages: (1) high-quality, innovative products and technologies; (2) global direct sales & technical support network; (3) application expertise and customer solution development; (4) global manufacturing reach. Leadership position in multiple segments through continuous product innovation and customer-centric approach.

## Supply chain — ความเสี่ยง
**Key risks:** Raw materials, components, and sourcing from suppliers; tariff/trade policy exposure (US tariffs on China, Mexico imports); geopolitical tensions (Europe/Middle East) impacting supply chain and raw material prices; difficulty passing cost increases to customers due to competitive pricing; currency volatility (67% sales in non-USD); potential disruptions from natural disasters, conflict, pandemics affecting facilities and distribution.

## Seasonality
Highest sales volume in second half of fiscal year due to timing of customer capital spending programs. Lowest in Q1 due to customer holiday shutdowns.

## Human capital
Approximately 8,000 employees worldwide. Principal manufacturing in US (Ohio, Georgia, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Illinois, Michigan, Minnesota, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Tennessee, Florida, Texas, Alabama, Wisconsin) and internationally (China, Bulgaria, Germany, Ireland, India, Israel, Italy, Mexico, Netherlands, UK). Total rewards program includes competitive wages, health/welfare benefits, retirement plans (401k match 50¢/$1 up to 6% contribution; enhanced 3% for hires post-7/2021), pension plans, and recognition programs. Compensation emphasis on pay-for-performance and employee wellbeing.

## IP
Global portfolio of 2,100+ granted and pending patents and 1,000+ trademarks. Relies on patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, confidentiality and IP agreements with employees. Consider competitive advantage driven more by technical, marketing, and sales competence of employees than any single patent or trademark.

## FY2025 Financial Summary

### Revenue drivers
- **Total revenue $2,791.7M, +3.8% YoY** (FY2024: $2,689.9M; FY2023: $2,628.6M)
- **IPS: -4.8%** organic (declines in polymer processing and industrial coatings, offset by nonwovens/packaging/precision agriculture growth)
- **MFS: +20.1%** organic incl. acquisitions (Atrion acquisition; offset by Q4 divestiture of medical contract manufacturing)
- **ATS: +4.9%** organic (strong electronics dispense growth, offset by weakness in X-ray inspection)
- Geographic headwinds: -6.7% organic in Americas and Europe; +7.6% in Asia Pacific

### Gross margin
**55.2% in FY2025 and FY2024** (unchanged). FY2023: 54.2%. Gross margin flat YoY despite organic sales decline, reflecting flat product mix. Lower inventory step-up amortization from acquisitions ($3.1M in FY2025 vs. $7.7M in FY2024) aided margin expansion vs. cost pressures.

### Operating expenses
- **Operating profit: $711.7M (25.5% of revenue)** in FY2025 vs. $674.0M (25.0%) in FY2024; +5.6% growth
- **SG&A increased** due to full-year impact of Atrion acquisition, offset by lower non-recurring acquisition costs
- **R&D spending: $68.2M in FY2025** vs. $65.0M in FY2024 (2.4% of revenue)
- Severance/restructuring costs $19.3M in FY2025
- Divestiture charges: $12.5M (medical contract manufacturing exit)

### Liquidity & capital return
- **Operating cash flow: $719.2M** in FY2025 (+29.3% vs. FY2024: $556.2M)
- **Free cash flow: $661.1M** in FY2025 (+34.5% vs. FY2024: $491.8M)
- **Capex: $58.1M** (2.1% of revenue) in FY2025 vs. $64.4M in FY2024
- **Buybacks: $306.4M** in FY2025 vs. $33.3M in FY2024 (significant acceleration)
- **Dividends: $179.1M** in FY2025 (+10.9% vs. $161.4M in FY2024)
- **Total capital returned: $485.4M** (17.4% of revenue)
- **Cash: $108.4M** (Oct 31, 2025) vs. $115.9M prior year
- **Total debt: $1,996.3M** ($315M current + $1,681.3M long-term) vs. $2,205.1M prior year; debt decreased YoY

### Tax
**Effective tax rate 18.9% in FY2025** vs. 20.2% in FY2024 (130 bps improvement). Decrease primarily driven by decline in federal valuation allowances.

## Key Risk Factors

### Macro & geopolitical
- **Global economic slowdown / recession risk** — cyclical end markets (electronics, polymer processing, agriculture, metal finishing) sensitive to economic conditions
- **Trade policy & tariffs** — US tariffs on China, Mexico, Canada imports; retaliatory tariffs from foreign governments; uncertainty on future trade actions
- **Foreign exchange volatility** — 67% of revenue in non-USD currencies; material currency translation and transaction impacts
- **Geopolitical conflict** (Europe, Middle East) — disrupts supply chain, raw material availability, trade relations, customer demand

### Regulatory & legal
- **Data privacy / cybersecurity** — GDPR, CCPA, and evolving regulations create compliance costs and breach risks
- **Environmental / health & safety** — manufacturing subject to federal/state/local environmental laws; potential for material costs
- **Import/export controls** — trade law compliance, sanctions restrictions
- **Intellectual property** — patent/trademark defense costs; risk of third-party infringement claims; risk of losing proprietary technology

### Business risks
- **Acquisition integration risk** — significant goodwill ($3.3B) and intangible assets ($681.6M); impairment risk if acquired units underperform
- **Supply chain disruption** — raw material shortage, supplier allocation, cost increases; ability to pass costs to customers limited by competition
- **Talent retention / organizational changes** — dependence on executive team and key personnel; ongoing restructuring and cost reduction initiatives
- **Cybersecurity threats** — ransomware, data theft, business disruption from IT attacks
- **Product innovation / R&D execution** — must continually develop new products to maintain competitive position
- **Currency & interest rate risk** — higher debt levels post-acquisitions; interest expense increased 20.3% YoY to $104.2M

## Investment Notes

**Strengths:**
- Diversified portfolio across 3 segments and multiple end markets; no single customer >10% of sales
- Strong and improving cash generation: OCF +29% YoY, FCF +34% YoY
- Gross margin stable at 55.2% despite organic sales headwinds; pricing/mix discipline
- Debt reduction underway: total debt down $208.9M YoY despite acquisitions
- Significant capital return to shareholders: $485.4M in FY2025 (buybacks + dividends)
- Global footprint with 67% international revenue provides diversification

**Headwinds:**
- Organic revenue growth challenged: -2.5% organic growth across portfolio (offset by 6% acquisition/FX mix)
- IPS segment declining 4.8% YoY (largest segment at 48% of revenue); affected by macro softness in coatings, polymer processing
- Interest expense rising 20% YoY to $104M due to acquisition-related debt
- High goodwill/intangible assets ($3.3B/$681M) create impairment risk if acquired units underperform
- Macro uncertainties: geopolitical conflict, tariff volatility, FX headwinds, supply chain inflation
- Execution risk on organic growth initiatives (NBS Next framework)
