# Constellation Brands, Inc. 10-K FY2026 (excerpt — investment summary)

## ธุรกิจหลัก
#1 brewer and seller of imported beer in the U.S.; also a leading player in high-end wine and spirits. Beer segment (91% of sales) sells Modelo and Corona branded products across all 50 U.S. states under exclusive sub-license. Wine & Spirits segment (9% of sales) focuses on higher-end wines and spirits sold primarily in U.S. and select international markets. Operates 2 breweries in Mexico with third under construction; 9 U.S. wineries, 2 in New Zealand, 6 in Italy; 3 distilleries in U.S. Distributes through 3-tier system via wholesale distributors.

## Product lines
- **Beer:** Modelo (Especial, Negra, Chelada, Noche Especial, Oro, Spiked Aguas Frescas), Corona (Extra, Premier, Light, Familiar, Sunbrew, Non-Alcoholic), Pacifico, Victoria, Austin Cocktails, HOPWTR
- **Wine:** Kim Crawford, Robert Mondavi, The Prisoner, Mount Veeder, Ruffino, Sea Smoke, Schrader Cellars, Casa Noble wines
- **Spirits:** Casa Noble, High West, Nelson's Green Brier, Mi CAMPO

## Segments
- **Beer:** $8,315.2M net sales (91% of total) — #1 imported beer position, leading high-end U.S. beer brand
- **Wine & Spirits:** $823.8M net sales (9% of total) — repositioned to exclusively higher-end portfolio; includes mainstream wine divestiture (June 2025) and SVEDKA spirits divestiture (Jan 2025)
- **Corporate Operations & Other:** Corporate overhead and investments

## Distribution
Primary distribution via wholesale distributors in 3-tier system (distributor → retailer → consumer). In control states, U.S. government performs distributor/retailer role through alcohol beverage control agencies. Direct-to-consumer and on-premise channels also utilized. Top 10 customers represent ~60% of net sales, with single largest customer ~27.5% of sales.

## การแข่งขัน
Highly competitive market. Beer competitors: Anheuser-Busch InBev, Boston Beer, Heineken, Mark Anthony, Molson Coors. Wine competitors: Deutsch Family Wine & Spirits, Duckhorn, Gallo, Treasury Wine, The Wine Group. Spirits competitors: Bacardi, Brown-Forman, Diageo, Sazerac, Suntory Global, Pernod Ricard. Compete on quality, brand recognition, pricing, distribution strength, and shelf space/retail presence.

## Supply chain — ความเสี่ยง
- **Tariff exposure:** Section 232 aluminum tariffs and Section 301/Section 337 tariffs on imported inputs; retaliatory tariffs in international markets. FY2026 impact: $58.3M aluminum tariffs in beer, ~$10M in wine/spirits.
- **Packaging:** Glass bottles = largest cost component; 50-60% sourced from joint venture glass plant; long-term agreements with other producers; aluminum can and cardboard also exposed.
- **Agricultural:** Grapes from ~120 U.S. growers and ~25 international growers; water security critical for breweries (separate aquifers at Nava/Obregón); yeast and grains sourced for beer production.
- **Fixed cost absorption:** Decreased production efficiency from lower volumes impacts margins; in FY2026 saw $58.2M unfavorable fixed cost absorption in beer.
- **Distributor concentration:** Reliance on wholesale distributors; consolidation risk in distributor/retailer landscape.

## Seasonality
Beer: Higher demand during spring/summer seasons and holidays. Wine: Grape crush (Aug-Nov U.S./Italy, Feb-May NZ) creates seasonal inventory peaks; wine aging requirements mean large inventory relative to sales. Spirits: Year-round demand.

## Human capital
~9,400 FTE as of Feb 28, 2026 (includes ~1,100 through equally-owned Owens-Illinois glass JV). Approximately 20% of employees covered by collective bargaining agreements. Recordable incident rate (work-related injuries >first aid per 100 FTE): better than industry average.

## IP
- **Beer trademarks:** Perpetual exclusive sub-license to use trademarks related to beer brands (Modelo, Corona, Pacifico, Victoria) in all 50 U.S. states.
- **Wine trademarks:** Owns/licenses Robert Mondavi, The Prisoner, Kim Crawford, Mount Veeder, Ruffino, Sea Smoke, Schrader Cellars brands.
- **Spirits trademarks:** Owns Casa Noble, High West, Nelson's Green Brier, Mi CAMPO brands.
- Patents, copyrights, designs: Not material to overall business.

## Key Risk Factors

### Macro & geopolitical
- **Tariff exposure:** U.S. Section 232 (aluminum), Section 301/337 (imported goods), and international retaliatory tariffs. FY2026: $58.3M beer tariffs, ~$10M wine/spirits tariffs.
- **Foreign currency:** Peso volatility impacts Mexico brewery costs; euro/pound/NZD impact wine imports and international sales.
- **Consumer demand:** Economic slowdown, socioeconomic headwinds reduce beer/wine volume; FY2026 evidence of volume depletion.
- **Water security:** Breweries dependent on aquifer access in Mexico; climate change and regional water stress potential long-term constraint.

### Regulatory & legal
- **Alcohol licensing:** State/federal licenses required for production, distribution, sales; changes in regulations (label requirements, marketing restrictions, minimum pricing) could impact pricing power.
- **Tariff policy:** Future tariff changes (removal or expansion) directly impact COGS; retaliatory actions in key markets risk export demand.
- **Tax legislation:** State excise taxes, federal beer taxes, corporate tax changes affect profitability.
- **Collective bargaining:** 20% of workforce unionized; future negotiations could increase labor costs.

### Business risks
- **Beer volume decline:** FY2026 shipment volume down 3%; depletion also declining. Risk: economic uncertainty, competition from craft/non-alcoholic categories, market saturation.
- **Wine market depletion:** U.S. wine category experiencing broad depletion; company divested mainstream brands but organic wine volume still declining (bearish signal).
- **Distributor concentration:** Top 10 customers = 60% of sales; top customer = 27.5%. Loss of major distributor could materially impact revenue.
- **Tariff inflation:** Aluminum tariffs increasing COGS; ability to pass through via pricing limited by competition.
- **Branding:** Concentrated exposure to Modelo and Corona brands; brand perception risks (e.g., negative publicity, shifting consumer preferences).
- **Brewery construction:** $700M+ invested in FY2026 modular capacity; execution delays or cost overruns risk ROI; also assumes strong future beer demand (not guaranteed given volume declines).
- **Wine & Spirits strategy:** Pivot to exclusively higher-end wines unproven; risk that repositioning does not reverse organic declines or that premium segment contracts.
- **Supply chain:** Glass bottle sourcing 50% reliant on JV plant; aluminum tariffs and packaging cost inflation persist.

## Key Observations

### Strengths
1. **Market leadership:** #1 imported beer in U.S.; Modelo Especial = top-selling beer overall; Corona = 2nd largest imported beer.
2. **Brand power:** Iconic Modelo/Corona portfolio with proven pricing power ($128.2M favorable in FY2026 despite volume decline).
3. **Cash generation:** $2.7B operating cash flow; strong FCF supports capital returns even amid revenue headwinds.
4. **Portfolio optimization:** Divested lower-margin mainstream wines (June 2025), positioning higher-end wine portfolio for premiumization trends.
5. **Operational efficiency:** $200M+ in beer cost savings (procurement, logistics, efficiency initiatives) offsetting tariff and cost inflation.

### Concerns
1. **Volume deterioration:** Beer shipments down 3% (volume down $323M); Wine & Spirits segment down 51% (divestiture $711M + organic decline $133.9M). Volume trends suggest macro headwinds and category depletion, not company-specific.
2. **Tariff inflation:** $58M+ aluminum tariffs in FY2026; if tariffs expand or persist, pricing power may not fully offset (competition limits pass-through).
3. **Wine market weakness:** Divested mainstream brands but organic wine volumes still declining (shipments down 6.7% organically; depletions down 4.3%). Risk: higher-end repositioning does not arrest decline.
4. **High leverage:** Net debt $10.5B on $9.1B revenue = 1.15x net debt/revenue; interest expense $353M annually. Limited financial flexibility if consumer spending worsens.
5. **Capex intensity:** $800M+ annual capex (brewery modular builds) assumes strong future beer demand to justify ROI. If beer volume remains weak or declines further, capex could be stranded.
6. **Customer concentration:** Top customer = 27.5% of sales; top 10 = 60%. Distributor concentration risk significant.
7. **Timing risk:** Wine divestiture/SVEDKA sale completed in FY2026; organic wine volumes still declining post-sale. Question: will portfolio repositioning reverse trend or is category in permanent decline?
