# RH (Restoration Hardware) 10-K FY2025 (excerpt — investment summary)

## ธุรกิจหลัก
RH is a luxury home furnishings and lifestyle brand operating primarily through Design Galleries that sell high-end furniture, décor, lighting, and home accessories. The company operates two main segments: RH Segment (core home furnishings, outlet stores, and integrated hospitality experiences) and Waterworks Segment (luxury home fixtures and bath products). RH has established a differentiated retail model with immersive, experiential galleries that include restaurants, wine bars, and design services, complemented by e-commerce channels.

## Product lines
- RH furniture and home décor (Contemporary, Modern, Outdoor, Baby & Child, Teen, Beach House, Ski House collections)
- RH Rugs
- Waterworks luxury fixtures and bath products
- Design services and interior design consultations
- Hospitality services (restaurants, wine bars in select galleries)

## Segments
- **RH Segment:** Core luxury home furnishings business – net revenues $3,241M (FY2025), up 8.5% YoY; includes Design Galleries, Outlet stores ($290M outlet revenue), and hospitality venues
- **Waterworks Segment:** Luxury fixtures and bath products – net revenues $198M (FY2025), up 2.7% YoY; includes standalone Waterworks Showrooms
- **Real Estate Segment:** Immaterial in FY2025 and FY2024; results included in RH Segment

## Distribution
- **Retail:** 80+ Design Galleries and Outlet locations in North America; expanding European presence (Germany, UK, France, Spain, Belgium); one RH Guesthouse (Aspen; second planned)
- **E-commerce:** rh.com, rhbabyandchild.rh.com, rhteen.rh.com, waterworks.com account for significant portion of revenue
- **Waterworks Showrooms:** Integrated into select Design Galleries and standalone locations

## การแข่งขัน
RH positions itself as a luxury lifestyle brand differentiated by: (1) expansive, curated merchandise assortments; (2) immersive retail galleries with integrated hospitality creating non-replicable in-store experience; (3) design aesthetic and product transformation strategy; (4) omnichannel presence (retail + digital). Competes against traditional furniture retailers and specialty home furnishings brands on quality, design, and brand positioning rather than price. International expansion into Europe faces competition from established local and global luxury brands.

## Supply chain — ความเสี่ยง
- **Distribution network:** 4 RH distribution centers + 1 Waterworks distribution center (US) + 1 third-party distribution center in Europe; dependent on efficient order fulfillment and delivery
- **Inventory management:** High seasonal variance (Q2 peak for outdoor furniture); requires careful inventory planning to avoid excess stock or stockouts
- **Construction and development:** Building new Design Galleries requires coordination with landlords, architects, contractors; supply chain pressures on building materials and labor
- **International expansion risk:** Supply chain complexity expanding with European distribution; foreign currency exposure (GBP, EUR, CAD)
- **Product recalls:** FY2025 includes costs associated with a product recall ($1.4M in COGS, $0.5M in SG&A)

## Seasonality
Q2 (April–June) historically highest sales due to peak outdoor furniture buying season. Q1 (January–March) typically lower demand post-holiday. Q3–Q4 impacted by fall entertaining season but moderated by macroeconomic factors affecting luxury housing market.

## Human capital
- **Total team members:** 7,230 (FY2025) vs. 6,340 (FY2024) vs. 5,960 (FY2023) — growing headcount supports expansion
- **Retail/Outlet staff:** 2,400 (FY2025) vs. 2,290 (FY2024)
- **Hospitality staff:** 2,420 (FY2025) vs. 1,880 (FY2024) — significant growth reflects new Gallery openings and RH Hospitality expansion
- **Part-time staff:** 720 (FY2025) vs. 650 (FY2024)
- **Union status:** No unionized employees; no labor work stoppages

## IP
- Trademarks: "RH," "Restoration Hardware," "RH Interiors," "RH Contemporary," "RH Modern," "RH Outdoor," "RH Baby & Child," "RH Teen," "RH Beach House," "RH Ski House," "RH Rugs," "RH Guesthouse," "The World of RH," "Waterworks" — registered with USPTO and foreign trademark offices
- **Domain names:** rh.com, restorationhardware.com, waterworks.com, and others; perpetually renewable
- **Design patents:** Own design patents for select product designs (15-year validity)
- **Copyrights:** Website, Sourcebooks (design catalogs), and photography

## FY2025 Financial Summary

### Revenue drivers
- **RH Segment (+8.5% YoY to $3,241M):** Higher core business revenue driven by product transformation and platform expansion; hospitality revenue increased from new Gallery openings; outlet revenue grew to $290M (up from $258M FY2024)
- **Waterworks Segment (+2.7% YoY to $198M):** Modest growth; higher margin segment (52.9% gross margin vs. RH's 43.5%)
- **Total consolidated net revenues:** $3,440M (FY2025) vs. $3,181M (FY2024), +8.1% YoY

### Gross margin
- **Consolidated gross margin:** 44.1% (FY2025) vs. 44.5% (FY2024) — declined 40 bps
- **RH Segment:** 43.5% (FY2025) vs. 44.0% (FY2024) — declined 50 bps due to decreased core business margins and increased occupancy costs, partially offset by shipping cost leverage; excluding $4.0M asset impairments and product recall costs, would have been 43.7%
- **Waterworks Segment:** 52.9% (FY2025) vs. 52.7% (FY2024) — improved 20 bps

### Operating expenses
- **SG&A expenses:** $1,128M (32.8% of revenue) vs. $1,092M (34.4%) — increased $36M absolute but improved as % of revenue
  - RH Segment SG&A: $1,046M (32.3% of RH revenue) vs. $1,016M (34.0%) — decreased 170 bps as % of revenue, driven by leverage from advertising and occupancy cost reductions
  - Waterworks SG&A: $83M (41.8% of revenue) vs. $77M (39.7%) — increased 210 bps due to higher compensation and corporate costs

### Liquidity & capital return
- **Operating cash flow:** $452M (FY2025) vs. $17M (FY2024) — dramatic improvement; includes $214M inventory reduction benefit
- **Capex/Investing:** ($224M) — capital expenditures for Gallery expansion, infrastructure, and technology; adjusted capex guidance FY2026: $240–260M
- **Free cash flow:** ~$228M after capex
- **Debt management:** Total debt reduced from $2,624M to $2,419M; net debt reduced from $2,594M to $2,378M
- **Share repurchases:** Suspended in FY2025 due to focusing capital on debt reduction; $201M remains authorized under Share Repurchase Program
- **No dividend:** Company does not pay dividends; expects no cash dividends in foreseeable future

### Tax
- **Effective tax rate:** 28.2% (FY2025) vs. 5.4% (FY2024) — significant increase due to discrete tax items and lower pre-tax income adjustments in FY2024
- **Income tax expense:** $47.2M (FY2025) vs. $4.8M (FY2024)
- **Note:** Company subject to 1% excise tax on share repurchases under Inflation Reduction Act and subject to potential corporate alternative minimum tax (15%) for corporations with >$1B average adjusted financial income over 3 years (not material impact FY2025/FY2024)

## Key Risk Factors

### Macro & geopolitical
- **Consumer spending and economic conditions:** Luxury home furnishings highly sensitive to discretionary consumer spending; vulnerable to recessions, elevated interest rates, mortgage rate increases, and housing market weakness
- **Inflation and construction costs:** Ongoing inflation impacts product sourcing, labor, and Gallery construction; delays and cost overruns on new site development
- **Interest rate risk:** Higher interest rates increase borrowing costs on variable-rate debt (ABL facility, Term Loans); every 100 bps interest rate change impacts annual interest expense by ~$24M; reduced consumer demand for home purchases in high-rate environment
- **Foreign currency exposure:** Expansion into Europe (GBP, EUR, CAD) creates translation and transaction exposure as revenues and expenses are denominated in foreign currencies

### Regulatory & legal
- **Compliance:** Subject to labor, customs, advertising, consumer protection, privacy, safety, real estate, environmental, and zoning regulations across US and international markets
- **1% excise tax on share repurchases:** Applies to future repurchase programs; reduces capital allocation flexibility
- **Litigation:** No material ongoing legal proceedings disclosed

### Business risks
- **Supply chain and fulfillment:** Efficient operation of 4 US distribution centers, 1 Waterworks distribution center, home delivery centers, and European third-party facilities critical to meeting demand; product recalls or delivery failures harm customer satisfaction and margins
- **International expansion execution:** Multiple Gallery openings planned in Europe; risks include landlord coordination delays, construction cost overruns, market entry challenges, unfamiliar competitive dynamics, local regulatory complexities
- **Competition:** Faces competition from traditional furniture retailers, specialty home furnishings brands, and e-commerce players; differentiation through design, quality, and experiential retail is ongoing
- **Key personnel:** Dependent on talented senior leadership and retail staff; inability to attract/retain qualified personnel may impact operations
- **Seasonality and demand volatility:** Q2 peak season for outdoor furniture; broader macro sensitivity to housing market; inventory planning risk if demand swings sharply
