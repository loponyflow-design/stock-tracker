# Arista Networks 10-K FY2025 (excerpt — investment summary)

## ธุรกิจหลัก
Arista is an industry leader in data-driven, client-to-cloud networking for large AI, data center, campus and routing environments. The company designs and sells high-performance networking infrastructure (Ethernet switches and routers) powered by its proprietary EOS operating system and network architecture, enabling customers to build scalable, automated data center and enterprise networks. Arista controls the full technology stack from hardware to software, providing integrated solutions without vendor lock-in.

## Product lines
- **Cloud/AI Networking ("AI Center")**: High-speed Ethernet switching platforms for AI workloads (leaf-spine architecture); Scale Out solutions for multi-rack training/inference clusters; Scale Across for distributed geographic clusters
- **Campus & Routing**: Modular and fixed campus spine switches, Power-over-Ethernet (PoE) leaf switches, Wi-Fi access points, managed through CloudVision; Cloud-Grade Routing platforms (7280R4, 7500R3, 7800R4); VeloCloud SD-WAN for branch connectivity
- **Software & Services**: EOS network operating system, CloudVision management platform, Arista NetDL (Network Data Lake), A-Care support services, AI Ops, observability, zero trust networking

## Segments
Arista reports revenue by geography rather than product segments:
- **Americas**: $7,122.1M (79.1% of FY2025 revenue) — includes North America and South America
- **Europe, Middle East and Africa (EMEA)**: $1,070.3M (11.9%)
- **Asia-Pacific (APAC)**: $813.3M (9.0%)

Product mix: Ethernet switches and routing hardware represent 84.1% of revenue; service and support (PCS, professional services) represent 15.9%.

## Distribution
Arista sells through direct sales force and channel partners. Large customers (cloud hyperscalers, AI infrastructure providers) typically receive direct pricing and account management. Enterprise customers access products via channel partners, systems integrators, and resellers. Multi-year service contracts (Professional and Consulting Services — PCS) are growing component of revenue.

## การแข่งขัน
Arista is a leading independent vendor in high-speed Ethernet switching. Key competitive advantages: (1) open standards-based architecture avoiding proprietary lock-in; (2) advanced real-time telemetry and intelligent automation; (3) deep expertise in hyperscale/AI workloads (leaf-spine, high-density, high-speed fabric); (4) strong relationships with major cloud and AI customers. Market is consolidating but Arista maintains position through technical leadership and customer relationships. Faces competition from larger integrated vendors (Cisco, Juniper) but differentiation in AI/cloud segments is strong.

## Supply chain — ความเสี่ยง
- **Component sourcing**: Key merchant silicon chips from single supplier; many components (ICs, power supplies) from sole or limited sources; reliance on contract manufacturers in Malaysia, Vietnam, Mexico
- **Tariff/trade risk**: Products manufactured internationally; subject to U.S. tariffs on imports; potential tariff escalation on China-sourced components
- **Lead time/shortages**: Industry volatility in semiconductor availability; potential supply disruptions could delay customer shipments
- **Large customer concentration**: Top customers represent material portion of revenue; customer capital expenditure cycles create volatility

## Seasonality
Not explicitly disclosed. Typical networking equipment: demand driven by customer budget cycles and capital project timing rather than calendar seasonality. Large AI/cloud customer orders can be lumpy and defer between quarters.

## Human capital
As of December 31, 2025: approximately 5,115 full-time employees worldwide. No union representation. Mix of roles: R&D engineers, customer engineering, sales, marketing, operations. Company received recognition in 2025 from Forbes (America's Best Companies), TIME (World's Best Companies), and awards for culture and leadership. CEO and CFO are both female. Strong focus on AI skills training and upskilling across organization.

## IP
Arista owns portfolio of U.S. and foreign patents protecting EOS operating system, switching architectures, networking algorithms. Core trademarks: "Arista," "EOS," "CloudVision." Patents typically have 20-year duration from filing. Company licenses open-source and third-party software. IP strategy focused on continued patent filings to protect innovations in AI-optimized networking, network observability, and automation.

## Key Risk Factors

### Macro & geopolitical
- **Tariff/trade uncertainty:** Products manufactured in Malaysia, Vietnam, Mexico; exposed to U.S. tariffs on imports. Further escalation of U.S.-China trade tensions could increase material costs or force supply chain restructuring
- **Macroeconomic slowdown:** If customer AI spending cycles reverse or enterprise IT budgets contract, revenue growth could materially slow
- **Geopolitical instability:** Supply chain disruptions from regional conflicts; potential export controls on advanced semiconductors; risk to international operations

### Regulatory & legal
- **Export controls:** U.S. regulations on semiconductor/networking technology sales to certain countries could restrict addressable market (e.g., restrictions on advanced chips to China)
- **IP litigation risk:** Large patent portfolios in industry create patent dispute risk; Arista could face claims from competitors or non-practicing entities
- **Data privacy/security:** Expanding GDPR compliance obligations in Europe; potential regulatory pressure on networking infrastructure vendors regarding data residency or lawful access

### Business risks
- **Customer concentration:** Large cloud/AI customers represent material revenue; loss of major customer or reduction in their capex could be significant headwind
- **Supply chain dependency:** Sole-source merchant silicon supplier; potential disruption could halt product shipments; contract manufacturer geopolitical risks
- **Competitive intensity:** Larger vendors (Cisco, Juniper) or new entrants could erode market share; Arista must continuously innovate to maintain lead
- **AI cycle risk:** If AI workload demand plateaus or consolidates to fewer large players, demand for scale-out switching could moderate
- **Execution risk:** Rapid product expansion (Scale Out, Scale Across, campus, routing) requires successful R&D execution and market adoption
